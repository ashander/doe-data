New York Nuclear Profile                                     
2008 Profiles

State Overview

There are four operating nuclear power plants in New York:

    James A. Fitzpatrick in Oswego County
  • FitzPatrick is now owned by Entergy Nuclear FitzPatrick.
  • The FitzPatrick reactor has a Mark 1 BWR containment design. Two thirds of the Nation's BWRs have Mark 1 containment designs.
  • On September 8, 2008, the Nuclear Regulatory Commission approved a 20-year license extension for this plant.
    Robert E. Ginna in Wayne County
  • The Ginna reactor is the smallest of the six Westinghouse two-loop pressurized water reactors in service. The others are at Point Beach, Kewaunee, and Prairie Island.
  • On November 25, 2003, Constellation Energy Group announced its acquisition of the Ginna plant from Rochester Gas & Electric Corporation.
  • On May 19, 2004, the Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) approved a 20-year license extension for this plant.
    Indian Point (units 2 and 3) in Westchester County
  • Indian Point 1 was one of the first, large-scale commercial pressurized water reactors built in the United States.
  • Initially, unit 1 used thorium-based fuel, but the first fuel load did not live up to expectations and the operators switched to uranium oxide fuel.
  • Indian Point's first reactor, unit 1, was shut down in October 1974 because its emergency core cooling systems did not meet regulatory requirements. Unit 2 came on line in August 1974. Unit 3 came on line in August 1976.
  • On April 30, 2007, a license renewal application was filed with the Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) for Indian Point 2 and Indian Point 3.
    Nine Mile Point in Oswego County
  • In 2001, Constellation Energy purchased Nine Mile Point from Niagara Mohawk Power Corporation and other utilities. The plant's current owner of record and operator is Nine Mile Point Nuclear Station, LLC.
  • On October 31 2006, under Nine Mile's new owner, Entergy, the licenses for units 1 and 2 were extended by 20 years.
  • Nine Mile Point Unit 2 is a newer model BWR that uses a Mark II containment design.

Contribution of Nuclear Power

New York is one of the five largest states in terms of nuclear capacity and generation, accounting for approximately 5% of the national totals.

Nuclear power is less than 13% of New York’s electric capacity, but it produces nearly 30% of the State’s electricity.

Although many States with similarly sized nuclear industries are net electricity exporters, New York has historically been an electricity importer due to its high consumption.

License Renewals

  • Fitzpatrick: In 2008, the Fitzpatrick plant received a 20-year license extension until October 2034.
  • R.E. Ginna: The license for R.E. Ginna was renewed on May 19, 2004, and extended until September 2029.
  • Indian Point: A License Renewal Application was submitted for Indian Point on April 30, 2007. The license for Indian Point Unit 1 expires September 2013. The license for Indian Point Unit 2 expires December 2015.
  • Nine Mile Point: The licenses for Nine Mile Point’s two reactors were renewed on October 31, 2006. Unit 1 was extended until August 2029, and Unit 2 until October 2046.

New Applications

  • Nine Mile Point: A Combined License (COL) Application for a new Evolutionary Power Reactor (EPR) at Nine Mile Point was submitted on September 30, 2008.

Permanently Shutdown Commercial Reactors

  • Indian Point (unit 1) in Westchester County
  • Shoreham in Suffolk County

Operator: Entergy Nuclear Operations

Location and Service Territory: The FitzPatrick plant was built by the Niagara Mohawk Power Corporation near Oswego, New York. The 900-acre site is also the location of two other General Electric boiling water reactors: Nine Mile Point units 1 and 2. The plants share a common visitor center.

Construction Cost: $1.065 billion (2007 USD)

Staffing: There are about 650 employees at this facility.

Reactor Description: FitzPatrick has a single, General Electric Type 4, boiling water reactor.

Cooling System: Lake Ontario supplies the cooling water for the condenser of both FitzPatrick and Nine Mile Point unit 1. FitzPatrick uses a once-through cooling system.

Location and Service Territory: The Ginna nuclear plant is located on Lake Ontario, east of Rochester, New York.

Construction Cost: $346.15 million (2007 USD)

Reactor Description: The Ginna reactor is a Westinghouse two-loop pressurized water reactor.

Cooling System: Cooling water is drawn from Lake Ontario through a submerged offshore intake at a rate of approximately 354,600 gallons per minute. It then exits through a discharge canal in a once-through system.
