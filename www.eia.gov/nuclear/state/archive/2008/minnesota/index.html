Minnesota Nuclear Profile                                     
2008 Profiles

State Overview

There are two operating nuclear power plants in Minnesota:

    Monticello in Wright County
  • On November 8, 2006, the Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) approved a 20-year license extension for Monticello.
    Prairie Island in Goodhue County
  • On January 17, 2007, the Nuclear Regulatory Commission approved the license renewal application for the Palisades plant.

Contribution of Nuclear Power

Although it is one of the smaller states in terms of nuclear capacity, Minnesota’s nuclear capacity is nearly three times that of Iowa, the smallest state.

Nuclear power constitutes less than 15% of Minnesota’s total electric capacity; however, it produces nearly 25% of the State’s electricity generation, second only to coal.

Minnesota imports approximately 15% of its electric power use.

License Renewals

  • Monticello: The license for the Monticello reactor was renewed on November 8, 2006. The new license will expire September 8, 2030.
  • Prairie Island: On April 15, 2008, a license renewal application was submitted for both reactors at the Prairie Island plant. The license for Prairie Island Unit 1 expires August 2013 while the license for Prairie Island Unit 2 expires October 2014.

Permanently Shutdown Commercial Reactors

  • Elk River in Sherburne County

Operator: Northern States Power Company

Location and Service Territory: Monticello is located on a 2,100-acre plant site on the southern bank of the Mississippi River near Monticello, Minnesota. The site is divided between Sherburne and Wright counties. This is primarily an agricultural region.

Construction Cost: $455.8 million (2007 USD)

Staffing: The Monticello Nuclear Power plant employs more than 520 full-time and contract employees.

Reactor Description: The nuclear generating unit is a General Electric Type 3 boiling water reactor.

Cooling System: Cooling water is drawn from the Mississippi River, but from May through September, the plant relies on mechanical draft cooling towers. These towers enable the plant to meet surface water appropriations limits and thermal discharge limits during hot summer days.

Location and Service Territory: Prairie Island is located on the Mississippi River, about 5 miles north of Red Wing, Minnesota. The 520-acre site includes land formerly owned by the Army Corps of Engineering and privately-leased land.

Construction Cost: $993.3 million (2007 USD)

Staffing: About 700 personnel work at the facility.

Reactor Descriptions: Both of the units are Westinghouse two-loop pressurized water reactors.

Cooling System: Cooling water is supplied by Mississippi River for Prairie Island’s mechanical draft cooling towers.
