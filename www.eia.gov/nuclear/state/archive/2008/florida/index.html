Florida Nuclear Profile                                     
2008 Profiles

State Overview

There are three operating nuclear power plants in Florida:

    Crystal River in Citrus County
  • In December 2008, Progress Energy applied to the Nuclear Regulatory Commission for a 20-year extension of the plant's operating license.
    St. Lucie in St. Lucie County
  • The plant takes its name from the county in which it is located.
  • In September 2007, Florida Power & Light (FPL) replaced two steam generators and a reactor vessel head.
    Turkey Point in Miami Dade County
  • Although the Turkey Point facility has four electric generation units, only Units 3 and 4 are nuclear powered. Units 1 and 2 are fossil-fired.
  • Florida Power & Light plans to build two AP 1000 reactors at Turkey Point. More information is available in the Status of Potential New Commercial Reactors in the United States.

Contribution of Nuclear Power

Florida’s nuclear capacity is only 7% of the State’s total electric generating capacity, placing nuclear fourth behind natural gas, petroleum and coal.

Nuclear generation represents about 15% of Florida’s total electric power generation.

Florida is a net importer of electric power energy, importing 11% of its electric power use.

License Renewals & New Applications

  • Crystal River: Crystal River applied for a 20-year license extension in December 2008. The current license will expire in December 2016.
  • St. Lucie: St. Lucie was approved for a 20-year license extension in October 2003. The license for Unit 1 will now expire in March 2036. The license for Unit 2 will expire in April 2043.
  • Turkey Point: Turkey Point was approved for a 20-year license extension in June 2002. The license for Unit 3 will now expire in July 2032. The license for Unit 4 will expire in April 2033.

New Applications

  • Levy County: On July 30, 2008, Progress Energy submitted a Combined License (COL) application for two Advanced Passive 1000 (AP1000) reactors at a Levy County site, about 10 miles northeast of the Crystal River 3 nuclear plant. There are no reactors currently located here. If construction is approved, the work would begin in 2016 or later.
  • Turkey Point: On June 30, 2009, Florida Power & Light (FPL) filed a Combined License (COL) application with the NRC for two Advanced Passive 1000 (AP1000) reactors at Turkey Point.

Cooling System: The St. Lucie facility is cooled by water drawn from the Atlantic Ocean.
