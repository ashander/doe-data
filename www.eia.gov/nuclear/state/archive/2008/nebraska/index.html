Nebraska Nuclear Profile                                     
2008 Profiles

State Overview

There are two operating nuclear power plants in Nebraska:

    Cooper in Nemaha County
  • On September 30, 2008, Cooper Nuclear Station submitted a license renewal application to the Nuclear Regulatory Commission. The application is currently under review.
    Fort Calhoun in Washington County
  • Of the Nation's 104 licensed commercial reactors, Fort Calhoun's is the smallest in capacity.
  • On November 4, 2003, the Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) approved a 20-year license extension for this plant.

Contribution of Nuclear Power

Among States with nuclear power, Nebraska is one of the ten smallest in terms of nuclear capacity even though it has two nuclear plants.

Nuclear capacity represents approximately 18% of Nebraska’s total electric capacity but generates almost 30% of the State’s total electric power generation.

Nebraska is a net exporter of electric power energy, exporting 5% more electric power energy than it uses.

License Renewals

  • Cooper: An application for a 20-year license extension was filed in September 2008. The current license expires January 2014.
  • Quad Cities: The NRC approved Quad Cities’ license renewal application on October 28, 2004, and extended the licenses for both reactors until December 14, 2032.
  • Ft. Calhoun: A 20-year license extension was granted in November 2003. The new license expires August 2033.

Location and Service Territory: The Cooper Nuclear Station (CNS) occupies a 1,251-acre site near Brownsville, Nebraska.

Construction Cost: $1.152 billion (2007 USD)

Reactor Description: The Cooper unit is a General Electric Type 4 boiling water reactor.

Cooling System: The Cooper facility is cooled with a once-through cooling system fed by the Missouri River.

Location and Service Territory: Fort Calhoun Station is located on 660 acres near Omaha, Nebraska. The utility has an easement for another 580 acres, the acreage being maintained in a natural state.

Construction Cost: $754.65 million (2007 USD)

Reactor Description: Fort Calhoun is a Combustion Engineering two-loop pressurized water reactor.

Cooling System: Fort Calhoun relies on a once-through cooling system in which the water is not re-circulated after being drawn from the nearby Missouri River.
