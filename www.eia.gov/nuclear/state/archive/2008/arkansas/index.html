Arkansas Nuclear Profile                                     
2008 Profiles

State Overview

There is one operating nuclear power plant in Arkansas:

    Arkansas Nuclear One in Pope County
  • The Arkansas Nuclear One plant is one of the 100 largest power plants in the nation

Contribution of Nuclear Power

Arkansas is one of a dozen states with a single nuclear plant and accounts for about 2% of the national nuclear capacity.

In the state, nuclear power represents approximately 12% of total electric capacity but produces nearly 30% of the electricity generation, second only to coal.

Nearly all of Arkansas’ electricity production is consumed in the state.

License Renewals & New Applications

  • Arkansas Nuclear One: The license renewal application for unit 1 was approved on June 20, 2001. The renewed license will expire in May 2034. The license renewal application for unit 2 was approved July 1, 2005, and the license will expire in July 2038.

Operator: Entergy Arkansas

Location and Service Territory: Arkansas Nuclear One power plant is located on a 1,100-acre site near Russellville.

Construction Cost: $2.522 billion (2007 USD)

Reactor Descriptions: Unit 1 is a Babcock & Wilcox two-loop pressurized water reactor. Unit 2 is a Combustion Engineering two-loop pressurized water reactor.

Cooling System: Unit 1 uses water from Lake Dardanelle for cooling in a once-through system. Unit 2 also draws water from the lake but supplements the cooling with a 553 feet high natural draft cooling tower.
