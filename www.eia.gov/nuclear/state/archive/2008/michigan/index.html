Michigan Nuclear Profile                                     
2008 Profiles

State Overview

There are three operating nuclear power plants in Michigan:

    Donald C. Cook in Berrien County
  • The Cook facility accounts for approximately 36 percent of Indiana Michigan Power's generating capacity.
    Enrico Fermi (unit 2) in Monroe County
  • The prototype Fermi 1 unit was the world's first sodium-cooled, fast breeder reactor. It operated from 1963 to 1972. A partial fuel meltdown at Fermi 1 occurred in 1968. Fermi 1 shut down permanently in 1972 and was placed in Safe Storage.
  • Enrico Fermi 1 was decommissioned in 1975.
  • The Fermi 2 containment design is a Mark 1 BWR. Two thirds of the Nation's BWRs have Mark 1 containment designs.
  • On September 13, 2008, Detroit Edison submitted an application to build a new reactor at Fermi. More information on this application is available in the Status of Potential New Commercial Reactors in the United States.
  • Palisades in Van Buren County

Contribution of Nuclear Power

With almost 4,000 MW of nuclear capacity, nearly 4% of the national total, Michigan is home to a considerable nuclear power industry.

Although nuclear power constitutes less than 15% of Michigan’s total electric capacity, it produces over 25% of the State’s electricity generation, second only to coal.

Michigan imports approximately 2% of its electricity.

License Renewals

  • Donald C. Cook: The licenses for the Donald C. Cook reactors were renewed on August 30, 2005. The license for unit 1 will expire October 25, 2034, and the license for unit 2 on December 23, 2037.
  • Palisades: On January 17, 2007, the NRC renewed the license of the Palisades reactor until March 24, 2031.

New Applications

  • Enrico Fermi: On September 18, 2008, a Combined License (COL) application was submitted for a new unit at the Enrico Fermi plant. The application is for an Economic Simplified Boiling Water Reactor (ESBWR).

Permanently Shutdown Commercial Reactors

  • Enrico Fermi (unit 1) in Monroe County
  • Big Rock Point in Charlevoix County
