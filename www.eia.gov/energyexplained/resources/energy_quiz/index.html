Energy Quiz

Test your energy IQ with these 20 questions.

  1. How much of the natural gas produced in the United States during the first half of 2015 came from U.S. shale basins?
    1. 32%
    2. 43%
    3. 56%
    4. 68%

    The answer is 56%.

    The U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) estimates that natural gas produced from U.S. shale basins accounted for 56% of U.S. dry natural gas production in the first half of 2015.

    Learn more:

    • Shale in the United States
    • Where our natural gas comes from
    • Today in Energy articles on natural gas
  2. How much of U.S. energy consumption was from renewable energy sources in 2014?
    1. 5%
    2. 10%
    3. 20%
    4. 30%

    The answer is 10%.

    Renewable sources of energy supplied about 10% of total U.S. energy consumption in 2014.

    Learn more:

    • U.S. energy facts—consumption and production
    • Renewable energy—basics
    • Today in Energy articles on renewable energy
  3. How many gallons of gasoline are refined from one barrel of crude oil?
    1. 19
    2. 22
    3. 33
    4. 42

    The answer is 19.

    U.S. refineries produce an average of about 19 gallons of finished motor gasoline and 26 gallons of other petroleum products from one 42-gallon barrel of crude oil. How can one 42-gallon barrel of crude oil yield 45 gallons of petroleum products? The increase in volume is because of refinery processing gain.

    Learn more:

    • Refining crude oil—basics
    • Refining crude oil—inputs and outputs
    • Today in Energy articles on crude oil
  4. About what percent of U.S. electricity was generated from nuclear power in 2014?
    1. 5%
    2. 10%
    3. 15%
    4. 20%

    Answer is about 20%.

    In 2014, nuclear power plants provided 19% of the electricity generated in the United States.

    Learn more:

    • Nuclear energy—overview
    • U.S. nuclear industry
    • Today in Energy articles on nuclear
  5. Which U.S. state produced the most natural gas in 2013?
    1. Alaska
    2. Texas
    3. Pennsylvania
    4. Oklahoma

    The answer is Texas.

    Texas was the largest natural gas-producing state in 2013.

    Learn more:

    • Where our natural gas comes from
    • Shale in the United States
    • Today in Energy articles on natural gas
  6. How much of U.S. electricity generation came from renewable energy sources in 2014?
    1. 13%
    2. 26%
    3. 42%
    4. 55%

    The answer is 13%.

    About 13% of U.S. electricity was generated from renewable energy sources in 2014.

    Learn more:

    • Electricity in the United States—basics
    • Electricity in the United States—generation
    • Today in Energy articles on renewable energy
  7. How much of total U.S. hydroelectricity generation was from the state of Washington in 2014?
    1. 48%
    2. 38%
    3. 30%
    4. 25%

    The answer is 30%.

    In 2014, approximately 30% of total U.S. hydroelectricity was generated in Washington, which is the site of the Grand Coulee Dam, the largest hydroelectricity facility in the United States.

    Learn more:

    • Where hydropower is generated
    • Today in Energy articles on hydroelectricity
  8. What was China's share of total world coal consumption in 2013?
    1. 13%
    2. 25%
    3. 38%
    4. 50%

    The answer is 50%.

    China is the largest consumer of coal in the world and accounted for half of the world's coal consumption in 2013.

    Learn more:

    • International Energy Statistics—total coal consumption
    • China Country Analysis Brief
    • Today in Energy articles on China
  9. What percent of U.S. net petroleum imports came from Persian Gulf countries in 2014?
    1. 15%
    2. 27%
    3. 33%
    4. 45%

    The answer is 27%.

    In 2014, about 27% of U.S. net imports of crude oil and petroleum products were from the Persian Gulf countries of Bahrain, Iraq, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates. The United States gets the largest share of net petroleum imports from Canada.

    Learn more:

    • Oil imports and exports
    • How much petroleum does the United States import and from where?
    • Today in Energy articles on oil imports
  10. What percent of U.S. coal production in 2013 was from Wyoming?
    1. 15%
    2. 24%
    3. 39%
    4. 48%

    The answer is 39%.

    In 2013, 39% of total U.S. coal production was from Wyoming, the largest coal-producing state.

    Learn more:

    • Where our coal comes from
    • U.S. coal production data
    • Today in Energy articles on coal
  11. About what percent of the petroleum consumed in the United States is used for transportation?
    1. 90%
    2. 85%
    3. 80%
    4. 70%

    The answer is about 70%.

    In 2014, about 71% of the petroleum consumed in the United States was used for transportation.

    Learn more:

    • U.S. energy facts–consumption and production
    • Energy use for transportation
    • Use of oil
  12. How much did U.S. natural gas proved reserves increase from 2008 to 2013?
    1. 38%
    2. 49%
    3. 62%
    4. 70%

    The answer is 38%.

    As of December 31, 2013, U.S. proved reserves of dry natural gas were about 338 trillion cubic feet, an increase of about 38% over the proved reserves of about 245 trillion cubic feet of dry natural gas at the end of 2008.

    Learn more:

    • Natural gas reserves summary
    • U.S. crude oil and natural gas proved reserves
    • Today in Energy articles on natural gas reserves
  13. How many nuclear power plants are there in the United States?
    1. 104
    2. 99
    3. 61
    4. 55

    The answer is 61.

    As of January 1, 2015, there were 61 commercially operating nuclear power plants with 99 nuclear reactors in 30 U.S. states.

    Learn more:

    • U.S. nuclear industry
    • Nuclear power plants
    • Today in Energy articles on nuclear energy
  14. What is the largest cost component of the retail price of motor gasoline in the United States?
    1. Taxes
    2. Crude oil
    3. Refining costs
    4. Distribution and marketing

    The answer is crude oil.

    The cost of crude oil is the largest cost component of the retail price of motor gasoline in the United States.

    Learn more:

    • Factors affecting gasoline prices
    • Gasoline and diesel fuel update
    • Today in Energy articles on gasoline
  15. Electricity is called a secondary energy source because...?
    1. it's our #2 source of energy
    2. it's not as important as coal, natural gas, or oil
    3. we get electricity by converting other energy sources
    4. all of the above

    The answer is we get electricity by converting other energy sources into electricity.

    Electricity is a called a secondary energy source because we convert primary energy sources, such as fossil fuels, renewable energy, and uranium, into electricity.

    Learn more:

    • Electricity—basics
    • Electricity in the United States
    • Today in Energy articles on electricity generating fuel mix
  16. In what year were crude oil prices the highest ever recorded?
    1. 1974
    2. 1981
    3. 2008
    4. 2012

    The answer is 2008.

    On July 3, 2008, the spot (wholesale) price for Brent crude oil (the benchmark for international crude oil prices) reached a record high of $143.95 per barrel.

    FOB = Free On Board

    Learn more:

    • Spot prices data
    • Short Term Energy Outlook—Global Crude Oil Prices
    • Today in Energy articles on oil prices
  17. How much did the United States contribute to world carbon dioxide emissions related to energy consumption in 2012?
    1. 16%
    2. 23%
    3. 34%
    4. 52%

    The answer is 16%.

    The U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) estimates that the United States emitted about 5.27 billion metric tons of carbon dioxide (CO2) from the consumption of energy in 2012. This was about 16% of total world emissions of about 32.31 billion metric tons of energy-related CO2 emissions.

    Learn more:

    • International Energy Statistics—total carbon dioxide emissions from the consumption of energy
    • Where greenhouse gases come from
    • Today in Energy articles on carbon dioxide
  18. What U.S. state was the second-largest crude oil producer in 2014?
    1. Alaska
    2. California
    3. Oklahoma
    4. North Dakota

    The answer is North Dakota.

    About 397 million barrels of crude oil were produced in North Dakota in 2014, making North Dakota the second-largest crude oil producer, after Texas, of the 31 states where crude oil is produced.

    Learn more:

    • Where our oil comes from
    • Crude oil production data—historical data for the United States by state
    • Today in Energy articles on crude oil
  19. What U.S. state has the highest electricity prices?
    1. California
    2. Hawaii
    3. Alaska
    4. Florida

    The answer is Hawaii.

    Electricity prices in Hawaii are, on average, higher than in any other state. In 2014, the annual average electricity price in Hawaii was 37.34 cents per kilowatthour (kWh). In comparison, the U.S. average annual electricity price in 2014 was 10.45 cents per kWh.

    Learn more:

    • Factors affecting electricity prices
    • Hawaii state energy profile
    • Does EIA have electricity prices by state?
  20. Natural gas accounted for what share of total U.S. energy production in 2014?
    1. 11%
    2. 24%
    3. 31%
    4. 43%

    The answer is 31%.

    In 2014, dry natural production accounted for about 31% of total U.S. energy production. The equivalent of about 26 quadrillion British thermal units (Btu) of natural gas were produced in 2014, or about 31% of total U.S. domestic energy production of 87 quadrillion Btu.

    Learn more:

    • U.S. energy facts—consumption and production
    • Primary energy production by source for the United States
    • Projections for U.S. energy production, imports and exports
