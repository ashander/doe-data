Lit Floorspace in Lit Buildings

To analyze the use of different kinds of lighting equipment with data from the 1995 Commercial Buildings Energy Consumption Survey (CBECS), building floorspace can be described in three different ways: total floorspace in all buildings; total floorspace in lit buildings; and total lit floorspace in buildings. The latter two measures of floorspace with lighting differ because not all of the floorspace in lit buildings is illuminated (see Table 1):



Table 1: Floorspace Denominators Used To Analyze Lighting Equipment Usage

     (Million Square Feet)
1995 CBECS Total Floorspace in All Buildings:       58, 772
1995 CBECS Total Floorspace in Lit Buildings:       56, 261
1995 CBECS Total Lit Floorspace in Buildings:       50, 303
Source: Energy Information Administration, 1995 Commercial Buildings Energy Consumption Survey.

Within a particular building, different types of lighting equipment often illuminate overlapping areas of floorspace. For example, a 10,000-square-foot office building has 95 percent of its floorspace lit (5 percent of the floorspace is unused and not lit). There are two lighting equipment types in this office building--incandescent and fluorescent. The incandescent lights are only used at the desk for task lighting, while ceiling fluorescent lights provide the majority of the lighting. Only 10 percent of the lit floorspace is lit by the incandescent desk light, but 100 percent of the lit floorspace is lit by the fluorescent ceiling lights. In 1995, there was 50 billion square feet of lit floorspace in commercial buildings.
